The System Was Built
For Them. Not You.

Los Angeles County has more cars, more accidents, and more insurance steering than almost any market in the country. That makes what insurance companies do across LA County more damaging — and more worth exposing.

01
The Preferred Shop List
Your insurer recommends shops enrolled in their Direct Repair Program (DRP). These shops agree to prioritize cheapest and fastest repairs — measured by internal metrics called severity and cycle time — not your vehicle's safety or quality of workmanship.
02
OEM Certified Shops Are Hidden
Shops certified by your vehicle's manufacturer — OEM certified — follow manufacturer repair procedures. They exist across Los Angeles County. They're kept at the bottom of the preferred list on purpose. Insurers count on LA County drivers never asking for them by name.
03
The Out-of-Pocket Trap
When a non-certified shop and your insurance disagree on repair procedures, you pay the difference — on top of your deductible. In Los Angeles County where repair costs run high, that gap hits hard. Most drivers find out only after it's too late.
04
The Gold Standard Exists
A small number of shops inside every DRP network are also OEM certified — pre-negotiated standards that protect you from getting caught in the middle. These are the Los Angeles County shops we surface. They exist. They're just buried.
